Title: China Launches Tianwen-2 for Asteroid Sample Retrieval

1. China's Tianwen-2 spacecraft launched on May 28, embarking on a ten-year mission to explore asteroid Kamo’oalewa and comet 311P.
2. The mission aims to gather samples from Kamo’oalewa, a mysterious quasi-satellite of Earth, and analyze them to determine its origin and provide insights into asteroid formation and evolutionary processes.
3. Tianwen-2 carries eleven scientific instruments, including cameras, spectrometers, and a laser sensor, to study the asteroid's composition and search for signs of water or organic material.
4. After sampling Kamo’oalewa, Tianwen-2 will return to Earth orbit, use Earth's gravity for a slingshot maneuver, and head towards comet 311P in 2035.
5. The mission marks China's first asteroid sample return mission, following successful lunar retrievals, and adds to ongoing international efforts in asteroid exploration.
